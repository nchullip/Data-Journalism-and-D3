Correlations Discovered Between Health and Poverty Levels

This data was based off of data taken in 2014 from U. S. Census Data. We examined the outcome of obesity, smoking, and uninsured (healthcare) percentages at the state level based on three factors, poverty level (percentage below poverty line), median age, and median household income.

Analysis of Census Parameters

  • Positive Correlation between Poverty and Percentage of People Lacking Healthcare, Obesity and Smoking Levels
  • Negative Correlation between Median Household Income and Percentage of People Lacking Healthcare, Obesity and Smoking Levels
  • A strong downhill (negative) linear relationship between Median Household Income and Percentage of People lacking Healthcare
  • Texas has the highest percentage of people lacking Healthcare.Utah has the lowest percentage of smokers
